# Roadmap

Roadmap for pushing the product above the latest open-source coding assistants in its chosen market position.

## Position

Compete as:

`The best governed, local-first, self-hosted coding workspace for private repositories and engineering team operations.`

Do not compete as:

- the broadest autonomous coding agent
- the most IDE-native assistant
- the most generic chat UI

## Competitive Win Conditions

To stand above the current open-source field in this niche, the product must beat them on:

1. governance and trust
2. workspace management
3. private deployment readiness
4. team-safe coding workflows
5. stakeholder-facing engineering intelligence

## Priority Model

- `P0`: must ship to be competitive in the niche
- `P1`: strong differentiators that improve adoption and pricing power
- `P2`: platform expansion and longer-term moat

## What “Above The Latest OSS Coding Assistants” Means Here

It does not mean beating every competitor at every dimension.

It means winning a narrower but valuable category with a product that is:

- safer than generic coding agents
- more structured than terminal pair-programming tools
- more business-readable than editor-native assistants
- easier to trust in private environments than SaaS-first platforms

## Final Strategic Test

The roadmap is working if a technical buyer can say:

`We are choosing this because it gives us self-hosted coding assistance with stronger governance, better workspace control, and better reporting than the usual open-source assistants.`
